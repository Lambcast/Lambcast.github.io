Welcome

I'm Alan Lamb, an applied economist and data scientist completing my M.S. in Applied Economics at the University of Maryland. I specialize in forecasting and causal inference — building models that don't just describe what happened, but identify why, and predict what comes next.

This site is where I publish that work: time-series forecasts, causal evaluations, and the Python and R pipelines behind them.

What I Do

I build forecasting pipelines in Python and conduct causal inference analyses in R — difference-in-differences, event studies, and synthetic control designs applied to real economic questions. I work with public data across financial markets, labor markets, commodities, and policy domains, and document everything end-to-end so the methodology is as visible as the result.

My models are built to generalize: the same rigorous approach to uncertainty quantification, factor selection, and calibration applies whether the question is a commodity price, a demand forecast, or a policy evaluation.
