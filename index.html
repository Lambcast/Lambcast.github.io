Welcome

I'm Alan Lamb, an applied economist and data scientist completing my M.S. in Applied Economics at the University of Maryland. I specialize in forecasting and causal inference — building models that don't just describe what happened, but identify why, and predict what comes next.

This site is where I publish that work: time-series forecasts, policy evaluations, and the Python and R pipelines behind them.

What I Do

I build forecasting pipelines in Python and conduct causal inference analyses in R — difference-in-differences, event studies, and synthetic control designs applied to real economic questions. I work with public data from FRED, BLS, EIA, and Census sources, and document everything end-to-end so the methodology is as visible as the result.

My focus is the Mountain West economy: labor markets, housing, energy, and natural resource dynamics — domains where good data work is scarce and the questions are genuinely hard.

Latest Work

Currently in progress: a Montana housing price forecast built with Meta's Prophet library, and a causal evaluation of regional labor market responses to energy sector shifts. Code published on GitHub. Write-ups posted here as projects complete.
