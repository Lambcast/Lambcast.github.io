Who I Am

I'm Alan Lamb, an applied economist and data scientist based in Billings, Montana. I'm completing my M.S. in Applied Economics at the University of Maryland, with a focus on forecasting, causal inference, and data-driven policy evaluation. I also hold an MPA from the University of Montana.

Before economics, I spent 14 years managing bars and restaurants — which taught me more about demand forecasting, operational decisions under uncertainty, and managing people than most classrooms do.

What I Work On

I build forecasting pipelines in Python and causal inference models in R. On the forecasting side, I use tools like Prophet and Statsmodels to model time-series data — housing prices, energy indicators, regional economic trends. On the causal side, I use difference-in-differences, event studies, and synthetic control designs to evaluate policies and natural experiments.

My data work lives in SQL, Python, and R, and I publish everything to GitHub so the methodology is fully transparent. My focus is the Mountain West economy, where rigorous quantitative work can make a real difference in how communities plan for an uncertain future.

How I Work

My approach is grounded in probabilistic thinking, calibrated uncertainty, and a preference for methods that are transparent and replicable. I draw on public data sources — FRED, BLS, EIA, Census — and document my assumptions so results can be scrutinized and updated as new evidence arrives.

I also participate in structured forecasting through Good Judgment Open, where I track my calibration on geopolitical and economic questions. That practice keeps me honest about the difference between confidence and accuracy.

Why I Built This Site

Lambcast is my public portfolio and working lab — a place to publish forecasts, share code, and write about the economic and environmental questions that matter to me. If you're an employer or collaborator, this site is designed to show you how I think, how I build, and what I care about. The work speaks for itself; the write-ups explain the reasoning behind it.
